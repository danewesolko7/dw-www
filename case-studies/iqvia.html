The challenge

Directly related to the increased demand of healthcare data several proprietary tools have appeared on the market. These tools connect users to data sources allowing for a variety of uses cases. While this model works incredibly well domestically, it doesn’t satisfy the needs of a global team who require international sets of data. Nor do the strict regulations related to the procurement of European healthcare information make providing a solution an easy feat either.

As an industry leader in Healthcare and Life Sciences IQVIA saw an opportunity to develop a new product providing users with the ability to perform bespoke research analysis of European real-world evidence. By creating a new tool IQVIA would be able to give clients a way to gain insight around clinical outcomes, treatment patterns, resource utilization, data source completeness and more. Working together with a London based internal product team I was tasked in designing a solution.

Working through

Prior to my engagement with IQVIA there had been an initiative to develop a new tool and productize services that are currently being offered. It was determined that this tool needed to:

  • Generate visualizations
  • Output research protocols
  • Assess data-source quality
  • Productize a service

Contracted as a User Experience Designer to lead the design my engagement:

  • Focused first on Health Economics Outcomes Researchers (HEOR’s)
  • Used company initiatives as the constraint
  • Relied heavily on research to shape a user centered solution

On-boarding and stakeholder interviews helped me gain insight regarding a concept I was given as the starting point. Internal review sessions assisted in gathering feedback which helped shape overall ideas. A prototype was created using Justinmind, an interactive prototyping tool which then was used as a vessel for gathering valuable research data. During the usability tests it was uncovered that users:

  • Did not understand the context of the application
  • Had trouble performing and completing tasks
  • Wanted a high level overview on the home page
  • Needed clarity around core functionalities
  • Did not comprehend some of the verbiage

Brainstorming and digital whiteboarding sessions using LucidCharts, a cloud-based diagramming tool helped in forming new ideas. Those improvements let us re-work the vision and were incorporated into a new prototype. During this process we established a minimum viable product and concurrently began the development of a proof-of-concept.

A major milestone had been reached when a fully functional prototype for HEOR’s was ready. The prototype was showcased at ISPOR 2018, a conference dedicated to health economics outcomes research where the product was launched.

Work commenced with the next two user roles—Commercial Pharma and Hospitals / Registries. Following a similar process we began by reaching out to subject matter experts regarding a general understanding of users. All three user roles had similar requirements so we were able to rapidly build new prototypes with the appropriate adjustments.

Having completed the assignment no further work was contracted, all deliverables were provided and the engineering team continued implementation of their proof-of-concept. Deliverables for this project included:

  • Low fidelity wireframes—ironing out concepts, layouts and page structures.
  • Interactive prototypes—communicating the vision and behavior of the apps.

    • The results

    Through our efforts we designed a functional prototype and proof-of-concept for IQVIA of a software application. This application will give users access to European real-world evidence and let them:

    • Generate a variety of useful data visualizations
    • Output previously performed research protocols
    • Assess the quality and completeness of data sources

    As as a result it is expected that IQVIA will be able to productize a service, increase its product portfolio and use the prototypes as a catalyst in reaching a year one sales target of $20 million.

    Looking back

    Being a proponent of user research if I was able to change anything about this project it would be how information was gathered. Significant work went into creating a prototype before ideas were tested with potential clients. Conducting user interviews upfront would have better informed our decisions and resulted in a user first design.
